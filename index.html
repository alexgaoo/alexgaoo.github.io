About Me

I am currently an intern in the Intelligent Systems Division at NASA Ames Research Center. I am also a researcher at the Autonomous Systems Laboratory at Stanford University. Previously, I have worked on independent research with Professor Mykel Kochenderfer, director of the Stanford Intelligent Systems Laboratory.

I founded and captain the Gunn Zero Robotics Team which participates in the Zero Robotics Competition. We program flight plans and autonomous decision making for the SPHERE satellites on the International Space Station for an annual problem of interest to DARPA, NASA, and MIT. Additionally, I am a controls lead for the Gunn Robotics Team, which participates in the FIRST Robotics Competition.

I was a controls engineer at rLoop Inc, which designed and manufactured a pod to compete in the SpaceX Hyperloop Competition. We won first place internationally in January 2017.

I have always been deeply passionate about space exploration, and I hope to utilize artificial intelligence to contribute to the field.

Research Interests

I am particularly interested in the fields of robotics and artificial intelligence, as well as aerospace and computer engineering.

Specifically, my research lies among:

  • Decision making under uncertainty

  • Autonomous control of space robots

  • Multi-agent control
